Measuring clinical communication quality.

CCQ develops analytic solutions that transform clinical conversations into structured, measurable insights—supporting healthcare quality improvement and research.

What We Do

Clinical care depends on communication, yet communication quality between clinicians and patients is rarely measured in a systematic or scalable way.

CCQ builds infrastructure to analyze clinical encounters and extract signals related to clinician–patient interaction and decision processes—using transcripts generated by existing ambient documentation technologies.

Our Approach

We focus on measurement, not judgment. Our work is grounded in established decision-science and clinical communication literature and is designed to be:

  • Encounter-level and scalable
  • Vendor-agnostic and workflow-aware
  • Suitable for quality improvement, system-level analysis, and research
CCQ focuses on measurement and analysis and does not provide clinical recommendations or decision support.

Who CCQ Is For

CCQ works with organizations that generate or analyze clinical documentation, including:

Documentation Vendors

AI scribe and ambient documentation vendors seeking to evaluate communication and decision-related signals derived from generated transcripts.

Health Systems

Provider organizations conducting aggregated analysis of clinical communication patterns and decision-related signals across clinicians, services, or care settings.

Researchers

Researchers and pilot partners studying clinical communication and decision-making processes.

Designed for Interoperability

CCQ is built to integrate with existing clinical documentation and AI scribe environments. Our tools are designed to complement—not replace—clinical workflows and existing health IT infrastructure.

About CCQ

CCQ is an early-stage healthcare analytics company focused on developing neutral, extensible measurement infrastructure for clinical communication and decision-making.

The company is led by a multidisciplinary founding team that includes a practicing clinician alongside health-services researchers and health-system operators. Together, the team combines clinical insight with technical and operational expertise to build scalable, policy-aware measurement infrastructure grounded in real-world healthcare workflows.
